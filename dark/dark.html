Gacy endured a difficult relationship with his father, an alcoholic who was physically abusive to his
wife and children. His father belittled him, calling him names as “Sissy”, “Mama’s boy” who would
“probably grow up queer”, and comparing him unfavorably with his sisters.

In August 1967, Gacy sexually assaulted 15-year-old kid. Gacy lured him to his house upon the
promise of showing him pornographic films. Gacy plied him with alcohol, allowed him to watch a
stag movie, then persuaded him to engage in mutual oral sex, adding "You have to have sex with a
man before you start having sex with women." Over the following months Gacy similarly abused
several other youths, tricking them into believing he was commissioned to conduct homosexual
experiments in the interests of scientific research, and paid them up to $50 each.

On November 7, 1968, Gacy pleaded guilty to one count of sodomy, but not guilty to the charges
related to other youths. Gacy was convicted on December 3 and sentenced to 10 years'
imprisonment.

Among other luring techniques Gacy would offer his victims jobs opportunity at his construction company, this method was applied successfully several times before his last victim, 15-year-old Robert Piest.

Robert was reported missing to the police by his mother who was waiting for him in the car while he headed out to meet and discuss with Gacy about a potential construction job. Soon enough a police search of Gacy’s house in Norwood Park, Illinois came and uncovered evidence of his involvement in numerous murders.

Over a period of just a few years, he murdered 33 boys between the ages 9-20 , 29 of whom were found underneath Gacy’s house ,26 in the crawlspace and 3 other bodies were found in other areas beneath his home.
