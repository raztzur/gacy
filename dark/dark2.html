Gacy endured a difficult relationship with his father, an alcoholic who was physically abusive to his wife and children. His father belittled him, calling him names as “Sissy”, “Mama’s boy” who would “probably grow up queer”, and comparing him unfavorably with his sisters.

In August 1967, Gacy sexually assaulted 15-year-old kid. Gacy lured him to his house upon the promise of showing him pornographic films. Gacy plied him with alcohol, allowed him to watch a stag movie, then persuaded him to engage in mutual oral sex, adding "You have to have sex with a man before you start having sex with women”. Over the following months Gacy similarly abused
several other youths, tricking them into believing he was commissioned to conduct homosexual experiments in the interests of scientific research, and paid them up to $50 each.

On November 7, 1968, Gacy pleaded guilty to one count of sodomy, but not guilty to the charges related to other youths. Gacy was convicted on December 3 and sentenced to 10 years
imprisonment.
After moving to Chicago, the “Killer Clown” began his killing spree. He would capture his prey, torture, sodomize them and eventually strangled most of his victims with his hands or with rope.

Gacy would drive around town and lured his victims in varies ways such as ; offering them marijuana, offering them to be employees at his firm, flash them with a fake badge pretending to be a police officer and then arrest them, he befriend them and take them to his home where he showed them tricks with “magic handcuffs”.

After he got his victims dead, he would bury their bodies in his crawl space as his house as he believed they were his property. He periodically poured quicklime to hasten the decomposition of his victims and to get rid of the smell. When he ran out of space he began to disposal the bodies in neighboring rivers.

Among other luring techniques, Gacy would offer his victims jobs opportunity at his construction company “PDM”. This method was applied successfully several times before his last victim, 15-year-old Robert Piest.

Robert was reported missing to the police by his mother who was waiting for him in the car while he headed out to meet and discuss with Gacy about a potential construction job. Soon enough a police search of Gacy’s house in Norwood Park, Illinois came and uncovered evidence of his involvement in numerous murders.

Over a period of just a few years, he murdered 33 boys between the ages 14-21 , 29 of whom were found underneath Gacy’s house, 26 in the crawlspace, and 3 other bodies were found in other areas beneath his home. Only 27 of Gacy’s victims were conclusively identified. Of these, the youngest were Samuel Stapleton and Michael Marino, both 14; the oldest was Russell Nelson, who was 21. Six victims have never been identified.
